## General Cybersecurity Tips

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account**
   Create passwords that are at least 12 characters long
   and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters,
   numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password
   across multiple platforms.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)**
   Turn on 2FA on all important accounts including Gmail,
   Instagram, LinkedIn, and GitHub. This adds a second
   layer of security even if your password is stolen.

3. **Never click on unknown links or attachments**
   If you receive an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message from
   an unknown sender containing a link, do not click it.
   Phishing links are designed to look legitimate and can
   steal your login credentials instantly.

4. **Verify websites before entering any information**
   Always check that a website URL begins with "https://"
   and look for the padlock icon in the browser address
   bar before entering any personal or payment details.

5. **Keep your devices and apps updated**
   Always install the latest software and security updates
   on your phone and laptop. Outdated software contains
   vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit.

6. **Be careful what you share on social media**
   Never post your phone number, college ID, home address,
   or daily routine publicly. Cybercriminals use this
   information to target and manipulate victims.

7. **Use only trusted and official Wi-Fi networks**
   Avoid using free public Wi-Fi at cafes, bus stands, or
   railways stations for banking or logging into personal
   accounts. Use your mobile data or a VPN instead.

8. **Do not install apps from unknown sources**
   Only download apps from the official Google Play Store
   or Apple App Store. Third-party APK files often contain
   hidden malware or spyware.

## 💰 Financial & UPI Safety Tips (Mandatory)

9. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone**
   No bank, UPI app, or government agency will ever ask
   for your PIN or OTP over a call, message, or email.
   If someone asks for it, it is a scam — hang up
   immediately.

10. **Understand the difference between sending
    and receiving money on UPI**
    To RECEIVE money, you never need to enter your UPI PIN
    or scan a QR code. If someone asks you to scan a code
    or enter your PIN to "accept" a payment, you are
    actually sending money, not receiving it.
    This is the most common UPI scam targeting students.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of any
cybercrime including UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying,
identity theft, or online harassment:

- 🌐 **Online Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in
  File a complaint anytime — available 24x7
  Keep screenshots, transaction IDs, and chat records
  ready before filing.

- 📞 **Helpline Number:** 1930
  Call anytime (24x7) to report cyber fraud
  especially financial/UPI fraud for faster response.

- 🏫 **On Campus:** You can also report to your
  college's IT department or student grievance cell
  for guidance and support.
